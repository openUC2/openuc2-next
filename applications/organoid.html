Organoid QC & In-Incubator Imaging

Organoids must never leave the incubator — so FRAME goes inside. Autonomous brightfield imaging of 36-well organoid plates with periodic complete scans, time-lapse series, remote control, and stable focus at 37 °C and 100 % relative humidity.

Imaging without removing organoids from the incubator

Organoids are sensitive 3D structures that require constant CO₂, temperature, and humidity control. Even brief removal from the incubator for imaging induces stress and can cause structural collapse. FRAME's compact body — together with its anti-condensation optics and sealed connectors — runs indefinitely inside a standard CO₂ incubator.

A LAN/PoE feed-through cable provides reliable connectivity without wireless signals, which are blocked by the incubator's metal body. Researchers start scans remotely from a browser, set up periodic full-plate scans every few hours, and receive alerts if an organoid detaches or focus is lost.

Key capabilities

🧫 True in-incubator operation

Sealed, corrosion-resistant body. Anti-condensation window. Sealed LAN/PoE cable port. Operates at 37 °C and 100 % rH without any components fogging.

⏱️ Autonomous periodic scanning

Schedule complete 36-well or 96-well plate scans every N hours. Set it once and FRAME runs autonomously for days without intervention.

🔒 Drift-stable autofocus

IR-based non-TIR focus-lock copes with thermal expansion at incubator temperature. Focus map per well handles gel height variation across the plate.

🌐 Remote control & alerts

Browser-based dashboard for start/stop, live preview, alert configuration, and log access — from your desk or phone, anywhere on the network.

💡 Low-heat illumination

Diffuse transmitted LED illumination with minimal heat output. Intensity is calibrated and feedback-controlled to maintain reproducible brightfield contrast.

🛡️ Safety limits

Hard Z limits (optical gate + mechanical stop) protect objectives and organoid plates. Watchdog restarts the acquisition software on crash without data loss.
