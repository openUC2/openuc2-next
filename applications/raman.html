Your upgrade path: brightfield today, Raman tomorrow

The most common scenario: a researcher buys FRAME for brightfield (and optionally fluorescence) imaging within a budget, with a plan to add Raman capabilities in the next grant cycle. The open cube architecture makes this straightforward — Raman components slot into the same cube framework that holds lenses, LEDs, and cameras.

The Raman cube accepts fiber ports (FC/PC or FC/APC), connects to any standard spectrograph, and integrates laser modules with interlock and safety shutter directly into the FRAME cube stack. A unified ImSwitch device layer allows synchronized image + spectra capture from a single acquisition script.

Key capabilities

🔵 DPC / FPM / Darkfield

LED-array illumination enables differential phase contrast, Fourier ptychography, and darkfield imaging — all without hardware changes. Available from Phase 1.

🟢 Dual-color fluorescence

488 nm and 635 nm channels with matched filter sets. Standard cube-based epi-fluorescence — compatible with the same objectives used for Raman.

🔴 Raman cube

FC/PC or FC/APC fiber ports for laser in and Raman-shifted emission out. Edge/notch filter set in keyed cube holders. Optional switchable BF/Fluo/Raman port.

⚡ Laser safety built-in

Laser module with key switch, emission indicator, safety shutter, and beam dump integrated into cube format. Class-compliant enclosure and interlocks.

📊 Correlative acquisition

ImSwitch device layer synchronizes image and spectra capture. Acquire a brightfield tile, switch to fluorescence, then trigger Raman at the same position — in one script.

🛒 Configurator support

The FRAME Configurator includes ready-made Raman cube templates with BOM, pin maps, and wiring guides so you can self-assemble the upgrade confidently.
