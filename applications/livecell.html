Live-Cell & Time-Lapse Imaging

Long-term automated imaging of living cells with environmental control. FRAME's open stage design accommodates stage-top incubators, and the REST API enables scripted multi-position time-lapse experiments running unattended for hours or days — inside an incubator if required.

Inside the incubator with FRAME

FRAME was designed from the ground up to support incubator-integrated imaging. The compact body fits inside a standard CO₂ incubator, while LAN/PoE connectivity replaces wireless links that are unreliable in metal-shielded environments.

IR-based through-objective autofocus (non-TIR) compensates for thermal drift that accumulates over hours at 37 °C. Per-position focus maps ensure every field stays sharp even when the sample drifts across a multi-well plate.

Key capabilities

🌡️ Incubator compatible

Compact body fits inside CO₂ incubators. LAN/PoE feed-through cable; no unreliable wireless connections. Sealed, corrosion-resistant finish.

🔒 Drift-free autofocus

IR reflective autofocus lock compensates for thermal drift over days. Global Z offset + per-position drift correction for multi-well plates.

⏱️ Automated time-lapse

Multi-position, multi-channel time-lapse via Python SDK or ImSwitch GUI. Configurable intervals from seconds to days. Queued job execution with error recovery.

💡 Low phototoxicity

LED illumination with intensity feedback photodiode. Short exposure times and adjustable duty cycles minimize photo-damage over long experiments.

📡 Remote monitoring

Browser-based control: check experiment progress from anywhere on your network, get alerts, adjust acquisition parameters, or abort mid-run.

🧫 Multi-well support

Programmable multi-well plate holders (6, 24, 96-well). Autofocus per well, per-well QC images, and resumable job scheduler after error recovery.
