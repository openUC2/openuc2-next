Teaching & Workshops

From high school physics to graduate-level biophotonics — openUC2 Discovery kits make optics tangible. Students don't just look through a microscope; they build one. Over 50 workshops delivered globally, from Jena to Addis Ababa.

[Photo: students at a workshop table assembling UC2 cubes into a fluorescence microscope]

Curriculum modules

Each module is a self-contained 2–4 hour teaching unit with learning objectives, materials, and assessment tasks.

🔍 Geometric Optics

Lenses, mirrors, image formation. Students build a telescope, a projector, and a simple microscope from scratch. Kit: CoreBox

📱 Smartphone Microscopy

Turn a smartphone into a microscope. Image onion cells, water microbes, and printed circuits. Kit: CoreBox

⚡ Electronics & Automation

Microcontrollers, stepper motors, and automated image acquisition. Students write scripts to move the stage and capture images. Kit: CoreBox + Electronics Add-On

♾️ Infinity Optics

Why research microscopes use infinity-corrected systems. Build one, compare to finite, observe the difference. Kit: + Infinity Add-On

🟢 Fluorescence

Excitation, emission, Stokes shift, dichroics. Image fluorescent samples on a system the students built themselves. Kit: + Fluorescence Add-On

📐 Light-Sheet

Selective plane illumination for 3D biology. Build a SPIM from cubes and capture a 3D stack of a fluorescent sample. Kit: + Light-Sheet Add-On
