How tele-histopathology works with FRAME

FRAME's large XY travel (130×90 mm) and robust autofocus allow users to scan a complete H&E-stained slide or IF-labeled tissue section in a single job. Tiles are acquired with hardware-synchronized illumination and autofocus at each position, then stitched into a seamless multi-gigapixel image.

Export in pyramidal OME-TIFF or OME-Zarr ensures direct compatibility with Napari, QuPath, PathML, and commercial pathology viewers. A joystick interface allows rapid navigation and manual review without a computer mouse.

Key capabilities

🔬 Full slide coverage

130×90 mm XY travel covers 75×25 mm standard slides with room to spare. Multi-slide holders available for batch scanning.

🧩 Automated tile & stitch

ImSwitch handles multi-tile acquisition, autofocus per tile, and software stitching. Output is a seamless whole-slide image with no visible seams.

🎨 Brightfield & beyond

Combine with IF/IHC fluorescence modules for multi-channel histopathology. Quantitative phase (DPC) for label-free contrast on unstained sections.

🖥️ Tele-pathology ready

Browser-based remote viewing. Send slides to remote pathologists or AI tools without converting file formats.

🕹️ Joystick navigation

Intuitive joystick control for direct sample navigation. Stage every step of the workflow without a keyboard.

📦 Open data formats

Export to pyramidal OME-TIFF and OME-Zarr. Drop files directly into QuPath, Napari, or PathML for downstream analysis.
