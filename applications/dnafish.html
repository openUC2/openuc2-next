DNA-FISH & Genomic Imaging

Two-color fluorescence in situ hybridization for barcode-along-DNA readout, chromosome locus detection, and spatial genomics on fixed samples. FRAME's sensitive dual-channel setup handles low-photon-count FISH probes across large tiled areas, with a one-button scan-and-analyze workflow.

Sensitive two-color FISH with FRAME

DNA-FISH probes are often dim — just tens of photons per diffraction-limited spot. FRAME pairs a low-noise sCMOS camera with high-NA objectives and matched Chroma/Semrock filter sets to maximize signal-to-noise on 488 nm and 635 nm FISH channels simultaneously.

Large-area scans over many cells are powered by FRAME's reliable XY tiling and closed-loop autofocus, keeping every nucleus in focus across fields. An integrated flat-field correction step eliminates intensity gradients that would otherwise produce false-positive or false-negative spot calls.

Key capabilities

🔴🟢 Dual-channel detection

488 nm and 635 nm excitation with matched emission filter sets. Simultaneous two-color FISH detection with minimal bleed-through.

📷 Low-noise camera

Back-illuminated sCMOS with sub-2 e− read noise. Detects single FISH spots reliably — even with dim probes or short hybridization times.

🗺️ Large-area tiling

Scan hundreds of cells in a single automated run. Multi-tile acquisitions with per-tile autofocus and flat-field correction.

🎯 Closed-loop autofocus

Laser focus-lock feedback keeps the focal plane stable across the entire tiled area — no drift between tile rows.

🔬 High-NA objectives

60× / 1.4 NA or 100× / 1.45 NA objectives for maximum light collection. Resolves individual FISH spots in densely labeled nuclei.

🧬 Barcode-along-DNA

Sequential two-color imaging supports barcode decoding along DNA fibers. Compatible with DNA-painting and Oligopaint probe sets.
